🧼 General Tips

  • Clean after using the toilet or showering.
  • Wash carefully around all folds.
  • Wash hands before and after changing pads.
  • Wipe front to back.
  • Dry before putting on underwear.
  • Wear breathable cotton underwear.
  • Avoid tight pants too often.
  • Don’t hold your pee too long.
  • Change underwear twice a day.

👧 For Girls

  • Menstruation is normal. Ask for help if confused.
  • Having periods doesn’t mean you're ready for pregnancy.
  • It’s normal to feel moody.
  • Talk or do fun things if you’re feeling bad.
  • Eat healthy and get enough sleep during your period.
  • Fertile time is ~2 weeks before period starts.
  • Cycles can be irregular for 2–3 years — it’s okay!
  • Track your period days.
  • Change pads every 4 hours or when full.
  • No need for pantyliners when not menstruating.
  • Wash with regular soap twice a day, special ones.
  • Use clean pads and change them every 4–5 hours.
  • Wrap and throw used pads in the trash bin.
  • Wash hands before and after changing pads.
  • If your skirt gets stained, cover it or tie your jacket around your waist.
  • Bring extra pads and tissues in your bag.

👦 For Boys

  • Circumcision is good for hygiene.
  • If not circumcised, clean under foreskin daily.
  • Clean smegma (white stuff under foreskin) regularly.
  • Wet dreams (nocturnal emissions) are normal for boys during puberty.
  • They may happen without erotic dreams or awareness.
  • Clean yourself, your clothes, and your bed after a wet dream.
  • Don't be ashamed. Talk to trusted adults like your parents.
  • Follow religious or cultural guidance after experiencing a wet dream.
